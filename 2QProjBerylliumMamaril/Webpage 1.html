Solar panels absorb sunlight using pholtovaltoic cells which generates DC energy and converts it to the electricity we use for our everyday lives. At night, the cells store the remaining energy produced into batteries.


There are 2 Categories of solar panels, the monocrystalline solar panels, and the polycrystalline solar panels

  1. Monocrystalline Solar Panel

    - Monocrystalline solar panels are sleeker in design and also more efficient.

    - These types of panels are called “monocrystalline” to indicate that the silicon used is single-crystal silicon. Because the cell is composed of a single crystal, the electrons that generate a flow of electricity have more room to move.(energysage, 2020)

    - This allows it to be more efficient, which of course comes at a higher price compared to the Polycrystalline panels


  2. Polycrystalline Solar Panels

    - Polycrystalline solar panels are also made from silicon. However, instead of using a single crystal of silicon, manufacturers melt many fragments of silicon together to form the wafers for the panel.(energysage, 2020)

    - Because of more crystals in a cell, they have less freedom to move; thus, the Polycrystalline panels are less efficient but less pricier than the Monocrystalline panels.

Possibilities In The Philippines

The Philippines is located near the equator, meaning that we get a lot of sunlight. Because of this, the Philippines is a perfect place to put solar farms. However, places like most of Metro Manila dont have the nice open fields ideal for Solar farms. However, this doesn't mean that we can't find another way around.

In SM NORTH EDSA, you will be able to see that they use solar panels on top of the building. The same can be done in our communities. However, these buildings where we put these solar farms have to be common in our cities or barangays. Examples of these are Barangay/City halls, Sports centers like Covered courts, and maybe even police stations.
